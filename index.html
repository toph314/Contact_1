The Whale Tale

A sailor from old Nantucket
Said, "Harpoons? Nah, I chucked it."
He met with a whale
Who flipped up his tail—
And swam off with all of his bucket.
The captain then cried with a yelp,
“That whale won’t respond to my help!”
The ship gave a lurch,
The crew screamed “Research!”
And blamed it on climate and kelp.
A gull flew above with a squawk,
And offered to barter or talk.
He said with a grin,
“You’ll never win—
That whale’s got the strength of a rock.”
So now when you sail with your mates,
Beware of the ocean’s debates.
A whale with a grudge
Will never just budge—
He’ll ghost you for nautical fates.
